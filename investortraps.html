Investor Trap 1 –Your Thoughts About Investing

The biggest threat to your financial independence is how you think about investing. There's a strong tendency to act according to your existing beliefs about the information. We're naturally wired to seek pleasure and ignore anything painful or attempt anything different.
When you process information, you accept it as consistent with your beliefs. We tend to see the world not as it is but how we want it to look!

The best way to make better decisions about anything is to learn as much as possible and understand what you are doing and why.

Investor Trap 2 -The Fear of Losing

The reason why you need a different mental approach to investing is because of your fear-based brain. The fear of losing is ever-present and a powerful emotion. Nobody likes losing. We all want to win. Right? To make matters worse, when the markets are in a downturn, it’s natural for anyone to panic and sell the asset.

Without enough knowledge of what you are investing in, fear is most of the time in control of your decision-making. If you have made long-term investment decisions that are very volatile in the short term, nothing will affect your thought process because you are investing for the long term. 

Investor Trap 3 -Using Recent Events to Gauge Future Results

Here is a typical example of how many people fall into this trap. A particular stock is dominating recent news headlines. It's all over the media, exploding in value, so you act on this new information and buy the stock. Did you ask yourself, is this a short-term or long-term investment idea? No. So, without realizing it, you have paid a high price for the stock and bought it at the wrong time. The stock could crash for weeks, months, or years!

These are typical traps many investors get caught in. Following the crowd seldom makes investors rich. Thinking short term is also a very different mental approach to investing long-term.

You must focus on the correct mental approach and forget about short-term interference to achieve long-term wealth.
Here is an essential long-term strategy to follow.

Balance your portfolio with different allocations of stocks and bonds. Sell some when they do well to buy other assets near the bottom of their cycle. Then, repeat the process. It does not get much simpler than that!

Investor Trap 7 -Investing Only in One Market Sector 

This is not only very risky, it’s also a way for you to lose a lot or all of your money. Should the sector collapse, you lose everything.

You know the old saying: Don't put all your eggs in one basket? Well, it’s true in the investing world.

Spreading your investments over many different market sectors gives you a higher probability of making gains over the long term and lowers the downside risk. Especially when you buy at meager prices and eventually sell when they are high.
